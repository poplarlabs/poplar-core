# Poplar Goals

Poplar is building a decentralized, economically-guaranteed property records protocol on Base, as the foundation for a longer-term vision of **groves** — real, land-based communities with shared governance and local economies. (Earlier drafts called these "network villages"; the settled name is groves, for how poplars grow — many trunks, one root system. A grove is a hometown you choose.)

## Mission

Enable communities of like-minded people — even far-flung — to gain real economic control: community lending, shared ownership, and a stake in what grows together.

**The bet:**

1. **Demand** — People want to commune with others like them, wherever they live, and get community lending and economic control. That is the draw.
2. **Flywheel** — Community lending attracts members and finances land acquisition. It is both the growth engine and the treasury-builder.
3. **Backing** — Gathered land secures the community's token. Real collateral gives the token a floor.
4. **Gateway** — Economically-guaranteed property records and data insurance are the trust bridge: they secure real-world land ownership on chain, so land can actually function as collateral and token backing.

Each layer pulls the one below it into existence. Nothing is built speculatively hoping demand shows up later.

## Top-Level Goal

Build a decentralized property records protocol on Base whose accuracy is economically guaranteed. It serves two purposes:

1. **The trust bridge** — it puts real-world land ownership on chain reliably enough that land can back community tokens and collateralize community lending.
2. **Early protocol revenue** — sold as data guarantees and data access to external consumers (crypto-native lending protocols first, TradFi via licensed partners later), it sustains development before the lending flywheel spins.

## Draft Goals

*The goals below are working drafts. They may or may not survive contact with reality.*

### Gateway Goal (draft)

Make property records trustworthy without a central authority, scoped to what an optimistic oracle can honestly secure and what loan underwriting actually needs:

- Bonded attestations with challenge windows and arbitration (lazy validation — cost is paid only when someone cares), launched where records are cheaply re-verifiable.
- Guarantee only objectively re-verifiable public-record facts — an economically-guaranteed title search/abstract (recorded liens, ownership-of-record), not title validity and not valuation.
- First-party guarantees from a fee-funded reserve; an open underwriter market only once there is volume to price.
- First customers are crypto-native (on-chain RWA lending protocols) plus data-API subscriptions; TradFi guarantees later via a licensed-carrier partnership. This revenue sustains development — initial build is funded by grants/pre-seed.

### Flywheel Goal (draft)

Stand up community lending that people join for:

- A pooled lending vehicle in a realistic legal wrapper (CDFI-style loan fund or a chartered-institution partnership; community capital via Reg CF, then Reg A), with loans underwritten via the records protocol.
- Capital is far-flung; borrowing is local — members participate from anywhere, loans concentrate in the grove's footprint (one licensing regime) and directly gather land.
- Loan repayment and yield from external cash flows (member income, rents, business revenue), so the flywheel is not reflexive.

### Backing Goal (draft)

Gather land and secure the token with it — keeping what backs and what anchors separate:

- Cash-flowing assets (the loan book, rental and productive property) back the community token; commons land is held in a community land trust — governed, never financialized, never liquidated for redemptions.
- The token is a security by design (Reg A or on-chain co-op shares), with redemption via notice periods and NAV gates that respect land's illiquidity.
- All community land registered on-chain via the protocol — the gateway proving its purpose.
